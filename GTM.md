# Go-to-Market Strategy: Credex AI Spend Audit

## Target User: Hyper-Specific Profile

**Not**: "Startups" (too broad)  
**Actually**: **CTO / VP Engineering at Series A/B SaaS companies (20-80 person teams)**

**Why this person**:
- Pain point: Their company pays for AI tools scattered across 3-4 subscriptions, costs buried in their budget
- Authority: Can recommend (to CEO/CFO) or implement (via engineering spend)
- Motivation: Make engineering budget 30% more efficient = career win
- Skepticism: Low (they deal with tool sprawl daily)

**Company Profile**:
- Revenue: $1-50M ARR
- Headcount: 20-80 people
- Geographic: US/Canada/UK (English-speaking, tech-forward)
- Funding: Series A minimum ($5M+) — have budget conversations

**Who Doesn't Convert**:
- Solo founders (too small, tool spend is $40/mo, don't care)
- Enterprise (procurement process, not driving decisions)
- Agencies (client-side billing, different economics)

## 100 Users in 30 Days: Channel Strategy ($0 Paid Budget)

### Week 1: Seed & Validation (Target: 10-15 Users)

**Tactic 1: Cold DMs to Specific Founders (Day 1-3)**
- Find 30 CTOs/VPs Eng on Twitter (Series A/B mentions in bio)
- Personalized: "Your Twitter thread about Claude spending caught my eye—built a 30-second audit tool. [Link]. Would love your feedback."
- Expected response rate: 5-10% = 1-3 early users
- Goal: Validation + testimonials

**Tactic 2: Indie Hackers Post (Day 3)**
- Launch in Indie Hackers "Show" category
- Title: "We Audited Our AI Tool Spend & Found $12K/Year Waste—Built This to Help Others"
- Authentic story: discovered overlap via manual audit, built tool
- Expected: 50-100 views, 3-5 signups

**Tactic 3: Beta Tester Outreach (Day 2-4)**
- Message 20 known founder friends / alumni networks
- "Testing a free audit tool, would appreciate 2-min feedback"
- Expected: 50%+ response rate = 10 users

**Week 1 Target**: 15 users, 3-5 testimonials for social proof

### Week 2: Community Engagement (Target: 25-30 Users)

**Tactic 4: Hacker News Share (Day 8)**
- Post to HN `/newest` Tuesday 9 AM EST
- Title: "Free AI Spend Audit Tool—Find Hidden Savings in Your AI Stack"
- Short description, honest about "Show HN" nature
- Expected: 200 clicks, 5-8% conversion = 10-15 new users
- Discussion reveals product blindspots

**Tactic 5: How to SaaS Slack Community Post (Day 8)**
- Share in #engineering channel
- Casual: "Built this after realizing we were paying for 3 overlapping Claude subscriptions. Free tool, no signup."
- Link: public share example (shows results without email gate)
- Expected: 100 views, 5-8 signups

**Tactic 6: Twitter Thread (Day 9)**
- "Thread: I found $2,400/year in AI tool waste. Here's what I learned."
  - Part 1: Most CTOs have no idea their AI spend (relatable pain)
  - Part 2: ChatGPT + Claude + Cursor doing same work (example)
  - Part 3: Recommendation engine we built (results screenshot)
  - Part 4: Built a free tool to find your waste
- Quote: Link to audit
- Expected: 2-5K impressions, 20-30 click-throughs, 5-10 signups

**Week 2 Target**: 30 users cumulative

## The Unfair Advantage (What Only Credex Can Do)

**Distribution Play**: 
Credex already has a network of sourced-credit companies + founders. **The unfair advantage is using Credex's operator relationships as a distribution channel.**

**Mechanism**:
1. User finds $2K/mo in AI savings via audit
2. Audit recommends: "Credex can help you capture these discounts"
3. User books Credex consultation (warm lead vs. cold outbound)
4. Credex negotiates AI credit deals → both parties win

**Why This Wins**:
- Competing tools must rely on SEO/ads (slow, expensive)
- We leverage Credex's existing founders network (fast, free)
- High-intent leads (already found savings, not theoretical)
- CPA: $0 (users self-select)

## Week-1 Traction Metrics (If Working)

**Target**:
- 10-15 audits completed
- 3-5 with >$500/mo savings (Credex consultation-ready)
- 50%+ email capture rate
- 5+ testimonials in Slack/Twitter

**Red Flag** (if this isn't happening):
- UX friction (form too long, rules returning "Keep current")
- No value prop clarity (users don't understand why they'd run audit)
- Wrong audience (reaching indie hackers, not CTOs)

---

## If Traction Isn't Happening (Pivot Plan)

1. **Verify**: Talk to non-converters (why didn't you book the call?)
2. **Diagnose**: Is it the tool (recommendations wrong?) or positioning (they don't see themselves as target)?
3. **Adjust**: If positioning, re-target at Finance Directors (they care more about spend). If tool, fix recommendation thresholds.
